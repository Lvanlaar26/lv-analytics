FAQ

Why a retainer model instead of fixed-scope project work?
  • Scoping is an expensive illusion: In four years of consulting, I've watched project after project burn weeks on upfront, building a plan that feels precise but rarely is. The truth is, you can't know the right questions or spot the real challenges until you're actually in the data. That scoping time isn't free; the client pays for it either way. The highest-ROI move is to skip the theater and start working. A retainer lets us do that — real progress from week one, with the plan adapting as we learn instead of being locked in before we know enough to make it good.
  • Data work rarely has a clean end: Once people use a solution, requirements change. Sources, processes, and reporting needs evolve, so continuity matters.
  • You keep control: If you need less support, scale down to the lowest tier or pause. If priorities return, scale back up monthly.
What happens in the first 30 days?
I start by getting hands-on in your environment — reviewing your Fabric/Power BI/Azure setup, existing reports, and data flows to see what's actually happening, not just what's documented. In parallel, we have a conversation about what's urgent versus what's nice-to-have. By the end of the first month, you'll have a clear view of what's broken, what's solid, and where I'm focusing first. No lengthy discovery phase — you'll see real work land in week one.
Can I cancel or pause anytime?
Yes. Tiers step up or down with 15 days notice, and you can pause the engagement entirely if you don't need active support for a while. There's no long-term lock-in — if the work isn't earning its keep, you shouldn't be paying for it. If priorities come back, we pick back up on a monthly basis.
How do we communicate, and who am I actually working with?
You're working directly with me. Day-to-day, we typically use whatever channel already works for your team (email, Teams, Slack), plus scheduled check-ins depending on your tier. No account managers or middle layers translating your ask into someone else's work.
How do you handle data security and access?
You control access at all times — I work within the permissions you grant, following your existing identity and security policies rather than asking you to loosen them for my convenience. Where it makes sense, I'll recommend tighter governance as part of the engagement, not just consume access. If your organization requires an NDA or specific security review before I touch anything, that's a normal part of getting started.
